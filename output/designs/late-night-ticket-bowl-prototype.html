Reuse from Existing Prototypes

  • The late-night direction contributes the reddish marquee glow, stacked uppercase title treatment, and cinematic darkness.
  • The ticket explorations contribute the perforation language, admission-stock silhouette, and ticket-stub archive metaphor.
  • The bowl remains literal instead of abstract, but its contents switch from posters to anonymous ticket stock.

What Changed

  • The draw CTA is now about drawing a movie, not rolling a reel or unfurling paper.
  • The in-bowl objects do not reveal titles before selection.
  • The winner state is no longer poster-first. Metadata lives directly on a collectible-looking ticket.

Design Intent

The product ritual gets stronger when the object before draw and the object after draw are the same family. This concept keeps suspense high, gives the bowl more physical believability, and turns the selected movie into a more memorable artifact than a plain modal card.
